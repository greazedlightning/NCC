Welcome to Newbury Cricket Club Local residents survey

There’s been a Newbury Cricket Club since 1822, and the modern sides first played at their home ground on Northcroft Playing Fields in 1947. Today we have three Thames Valley Cricket League sides, plus an occasional Sunday eleven, and a growing junior section catering for all ages from Under 11 upwards.

It’s a special place to play cricket, right in the town centre, with the river along one boundary, and Northcroft lane on the other, and the church spire in the background. In the evening the light across the outfield from a low sun setting beyond Northcroft Leisure Centre can be absolutely beautiful.

We are proud of our traditions at Newbury, and we like to blend competitive cricket with a friendly atmosphere on and off the pitch at all levels. We play to win and talent is rewarded with challenging league cricket, but not at the expense of the game’s traditional values of fair play and team spirit. And there is room for cricketers of all skill levels to practice and play in a team which suits their individual ambitions.

Our juniors are coached by level two ECB qualified coaches, with winter indoor nets for juniors and adults at the excellent Newbury College facilities, and summer nets and training at our own Northcroft cricket ground.

Winter nets - 2016

Newbury Cricket Club is delighted to announce its 2016 winter training dates at Clere School Newbury.

This year’s winter training will commence on Wednesday, February 10th and will this year be held at Clere School, Burghclere, just south of Newbury. We have chosen this venue for a number of reasons including cost, access at peak traffic times and also viewing areas for parents/visitors.

The nets will run every Wednesday night from 6.15 PM to 10 PM starting from the 10th of February until 16th of March 2016. After a two week break, there will be further net sessions on 6th of April and 13th of April.

We will have access to two full length cricket nets and space for fielding practice and other drills.

  • Junior nets
  • Senior nets

The junior nets timings will be:

Under 11s and Under13s only - 6.15 pm to 7.15 pm.

Under 15s only - 7.15 pm to 8.15 pm.

The costs for over 15s but under 19s will be £3 per session. The cost for Under 11s/13s/15s is paid separately and will be communicated to parents as part of the membership form process.

Senior nets (including under 15s who want a longer session) and under 19s will be from 8 pm to 10 pm.

The fees will be £5 per session. Concession fees will be £3.

New junior and senior members will be most welcome, as will ex-players and anybody that is interested in coaching. The club has also purchased lots of innovative and new training equipment which we’ll make use of during these sessions and the popular bowling machine will be available to be booked (details to be communicated) for players to practice their favourite shots or maybe the shots they need most work at.

We really look forward to seeing as many of you there as possible

Berkshire Natwest OSCA's 2015 Winner - Charlie Anstey

The OSCA’s (Outstanding Service to Cricket Awards) are aimed at recognising and rewarding the work, commitment and positive impact of volunteers. These volunteers give so much of their time to the game of Cricket and without these individuals the game would not survive.

This year, our own Charlie Anstey has been chosen as the winner of the OSCA in the Heartbeat of the Club category.

OSCA (Heartbeat of the Club) award is for that special volunteer that a cricket club simply couldn’t do without, often the roles they carry out are behind the scenes. They often undertake a number of different roles, and are always there to lend a hand.

Over the last 20 years and more, Charlie has undertaken a number of roles and tasks within Newbury Cricket Club and his actions have always had a positive and lasting impression towards the development of the club in all areas.

More about Charlie

He has dedicated a significant of time and commitment to the club and has always been willing to go beyond the call of duty for the sake of the club and its members. Through his overriding desire to recruit or retain players, the club has been benefiting from taking the senior and junior teams to greater strengths and achievements.

Join us for the 2016 season

Membership of our club is open to everyone, so if you would like to play in one of our teams or watch cricket in West Berkshire, why not consider joining, or just come down to the ground on match days and support your local team.

Senior Membership

All you have to do to join is read the Code of Conduct and submit the online Membership Form. Your application should quickly be approved by the committee. Ed Uden, Treasurer of the Club will then contact you with details for payment, either by cheque or by BACS if you prefer.

Alternatively you can come to a winter nets or training session at Newbury College as a trial. Bring your Membership application with you, and see how you fit in before deciding whether you would like to join.

The summer nets at our Northcroft ground are open to senior members at all times, but there is usually an organised club net every week on Tuesdays from around 6.00pm, when (weather permitting) you can be sure of finding players at the ground. Junior training is on Mondays, from 6.30 pm.

Overseas Players: Whilst membership is open to all, if you are an overseas player, having been continuously resident in the UK for two of the last four years, you are very welcome to join the club, but it may not be always be possible for us to select you to play in League Matches.
