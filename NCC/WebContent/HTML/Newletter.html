Chairman John O’Sullivan wins Berkshire Cricket Lifetime Achievement Award

Our very own Newbury Cricket Club Chairman, John O’Sullivan, has recently won a lifetime achievement award for services to cricket. John is pictured receiving his award at the recent Berkshire Cricket Annual Awards Dinner from Berkshire Cricket President Francis Neate.

This is an extremely prestigious honour which reflects favourably on both John and the club. The award reflects the significant contribution that John has made towards running and improving the club over many years.

View more »

In an exclusive Stag News interview, Paul Monger, who nominated John for the award, outlined the reasons behind his nomination.

"John pretty much performs every type of role in the club, and has done so for many years. He has been club chairman for several years now and combines this with being a vicecaptain of the 2nd XI, Team Manager/Coach for the U17’s, manages the annual Hockey Festival project, is the club’s main handyman during Cricket Force weekend and helps to head up the club’s development project group. That’s to mention just some of the things he gets involved with.

It is difficult to imagine how the club would run without him and the hard work he does goes largely unnoticed by members and parents as it has become 'business as usual', although John doesn’t complain about this or require praise.

In recent years John has setup a junior section, helped to improve the facilities at our ground, including overseeing the installation of new outdoor nets, and is a driving force in Chairman John O’Sullivan wins Berkshire Cricket Lifetime Achievement Award Issue 34 Dec 2013 the club when it comes to raising money to both maintain and develop for the future.
I would estimate the time John spends on helping the club at well over 300 hours per year. We were so happy when we were awarded Clubmark in 2012, because John had been the main catalyst for this to happen and his hard work deserved this."

First XI recover after poor start

The first thing I have to do in reporting on the first eleven’s 2013 season is to thank all the players. After being relegated the season before, the start we made this year, losing the first six matches, ought to have been enough to generate some serious discontent. But somehow it didn’t, and I think that is down to the love of the game and the irrepressible team spirit of the players.

View more »

Maybe that isn’t so hard for the core players who have been regulars in the side for years, but I was especially impressed by the attitude of the younger players, and those who couldn’t always get a place or who came in to help us out on the back of good performances in the seconds – people like Brad, Luke, Kevin Jeffery, Matt Corne, and Paul Mason, plus newcomers like Amalka, Phil Mundin and Billy Beaumont. All of these never stopped trying, and as far as I know never stopped having fun even when things were going badly As a result, the next eleven games – won five, drew three, and lost three - makes much more comfortable reading.

We have to work on our batting, that’s clear. I enjoyed watching Surya’s 70 against Teddington, JD and Millsy’s fifties against Maidenhead and Bray, and Luke’s consistent technique. Phil Mundin’s defence was always solid and Neil Farrow looked like getting a big score every time he played. There is talent in the line up, but far too often we gave away easy wickets, and more of our wins came from good bowling than from strong batting performances. But in the bowling we do have good reason to be very optimistic. Bradley started the season well with six wickets as cover for Rajeev and Kevin Jeffery, and although Bobby V wasn’t at his best early on, there is reason to think he will be back after a good finish to the season in the seconds. Kevin Mills consolidated his reputation as the kind of wily medium pacer who always gets wickets, with another 40 dismissals under his belt this season and consistent economy, going for under 3 an over. Corney, Luke and Surya all suggested they can play a part in the team with the ball as well as with the bat.

Kevin Jeffery’s return after his exams really helped us to restrict the opposition’s batsmen in the opening overs – he had the lowest run rate of us all at 2.82 per over, and took 15 wickets. His 4 for 18 from 14 overs at Falkland was one of the most miserly spells of bowling I’ve seen, and we really should have won that one for him after bowling them out for 132 and getting within 2 runs of victory.

So next year we may have a better blend of youth and experience, and the strength of the club as a whole means there are more players vying for first team places and a greater change over between the three sides, which is something we have lacked in recent seasons.

Finally, I want to thank Eddie for all is help as vice captain. A lot of what Eddie does goes un noticed, but he has been invaluable, helping with difficult mid-week selection decisions, turning up early to clean the clubhouse, opening the bar after every game, collecting match fees, and providing a thoughtful sounding board for tactics at difficult times on the pitch. He’s standing down this year, but he tells me he’s as keen as ever to keep playing, so I am glad to say we can look forward to seeing his Jack Dee smile out in the middle again next season.

Charlie Anstey



Another solid year for Second XI

If you had told me at the start of the season that, from our 2012 team’s top performers, we would be missing Heath Messer, George Thornton and Dave Tute for the whole of the 2013 season and only have Martin Walker available for 5 games, I’d have said that we would have had an issue with staying up, yet alone be involved in the promotion shake-up, but that’s how it turned out until the last 2 games. In summary, we missed out on promotion for a 2nd year running, but by a slightly wider margin this year, although we did beat both sides that did get promoted and got close to both of them on the occasions that we didn’t beat them, so it was more of a consistency and availability issue, rather than a lack of ability or skills.

We started the season with 4 straight 25 points victories, thanks largely to some fantastic batting and bowling performances, notably from Martin Walker and Paul Mason, included in my special stand-outs below, but also thanks to some good team performances. It wasn’t for another 4 weeks, however, that we would win again as the weather and some decent opposition started to eat into our lead at the top of the table and only 2 wins in our last 8 league games saw us eventually slip to fifth place.

View more »

Things that have stood out for me this season:

  • Paul Mason’s 2 x 130+ scores vs. Aldershot and Bagshot
  • Martin Walker’s 155 vs. Eversley
  • Maiden half-centuries for Amalka Naimanage, Tom Sanders, Sam Clothier, Billy Beaumont, Brad Alton
  • Continued consistent batting form of Simon Digweed, Martin Walker, Paul Mason, who all averaged over 30
  • 5+ wickets hauls for Bobby Vynatheya, Sat Pal, Martin Walker and Matt Corne
  • Excellent fielding performances of Brad Alton, Simon Digweed and Tom Sanders, who behind the stumps, has given us a lot more certainty this season
  • It was also good to see the new club members for 2013 getting involved, and Billy Beaumont, Sat Pal, Amalka Naimanage, Matt Bartlett, Mike Cullum, Dan Addicott, Umang Patel, Ben Robinson, Sam Clothier and Jovin George all played for the 2s in their first season for the club for at least one game
  • I also appreciated the flexibility of those that were playing for different teams on numerous occasions and Amalka, Brad, Mase, Bobby, Billy, Leads, Tom N, Ben, Sam, Dan, Jaymin, Austin, Matt and Nicole, all of their flexibility and patience is appreciated by myself and the other captains
  • I also really appreciated Jonno standing in for me on the numerous occasions that I wasn’t able to be there, thanks Jonno, you did a great job!

Finally, that’s not to say that I wasn’t happy to see all of the "normal" faces and I really do appreciate everyone who turned out and contributed All in all, I think we did OK, and better than I expected, but selection was a constant battle all season and I think we ended up where we deserved to be in the end. I’d also like to thank Charlie, Malc and Roly for their assistance in selection each week, as well as Ed and Jonno as this process takes so much more effort than anyone would imagine. I’d also like to thank Roly for some great wickets, I think Northcroft has never looked better than it did on some of those hot weekends in 2013, shame that we couldn’t match the surroundings with more consistent cricket, but as I said earlier, the ability is there, we just need to see it more often, both in terms of consistency of performance and availability.

Paul Monger
